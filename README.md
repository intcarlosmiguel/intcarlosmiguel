## About Me

I hold a **PHD student in Physics** from the Federal University of Ceará (UFC), where I developed a strong foundation in statistical mechanics and complex systems. During my undergraduate studies, I focused on **Network Science**, investigating small-world lattice models and extreme opinion dynamics algorithms.

My academic background drives a rigorous, science-first approach to problem-solving. I am deeply interested in the intersection of physics, computational modelling, and data-driven methodologies. ## Projects

### Machine Learning & Data Science

**[1. Fruit Classification](https://github.com/intcarlosmiguel/fruits)**
<br>
<a href="https://github.com/intcarlosmiguel/fruits">
  <img src="https://hips.hearstapps.com/hmg-prod.s3.amazonaws.com/images/assortment-of-colorful-ripe-tropical-fruits-top-royalty-free-image-995518546-1564092355.jpg?crop=0.982xw:0.736xh;0,0.189xh&resize=980:*" alt="Fruit Classification" align="right" width="25%" height="120"/>
</a>

> A Kaggle dataset comprising 7 distinct fruit species was used to benchmark multiple classification strategies. Each sample is described by geometric (area, perimeter) and colour features, including floral characteristics. This project reproduces and extends a published study that employed Support Vector Machines and Neural Networks, implementing both approaches via **scikit-learn** and **PyTorch**. An ensemble module is included, which combines the predictions of two classifiers through majority voting to improve overall accuracy.
>
> **Skills:** Machine Learning, PyTorch, scikit-learn, Data Visualisation

<br>

**[2. Magic: The Gathering — Neural Network Card Classifier *(in progress)*](https://github.com/intcarlosmiguel/magicneural)**
<br>
<a href="https://github.com/intcarlosmiguel/magicneural">
  <img src="https://www.hipstersofthecoast.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/08/teferi-time-raveler-banned-in-brawl.jpg" alt="Magic Neural" align="right" width="25%" height="160"/>
</a>

> This project applies deep learning to a domain outside of conventional datasets. Using **Selenium**, a web scraper was developed to collect 1,500 card images from [Scryfall](https://scryfall.com). The objective is to train a Convolutional Neural Network capable of identifying the colour(s) of Magic: The Gathering cards — a multi-label classification problem with imbalanced class distributions.
>
> **Skills:** Web Scraping, Selenium, PyTorch, Image Classification

### Data Analysis & Exploratory Research

**[1. School Dropout in India](https://github.com/intcarlosmiguel/india_dropout)**
<br>
<a href="https://github.com/intcarlosmiguel/india_dropout">
  <img src="https://github.com/intcarlosmiguel/india_dropout/blob/master/img/dropout.jpg" alt="India Dropout" align="right" width="25%" height="120"/>
</a>

> School dropout represents one of the most critical social challenges for developing nations. This analysis investigates a Kaggle dataset on dropout rates across Indian states, examining the relationship between school infrastructure quality and student retention. The study incorporates **geoprocessing** techniques to produce region-level visualisations, enabling spatial identification of the most at-risk areas.
>
> **Skills:** Data Visualisation, Data Processing, Geoprocessing, Exploratory Data Analysis

### Network Science & Computational Physics

**[1. Extreme Opinion Dynamics on Small-World Networks](https://github.com/intcarlosmiguel/Programas-da-Gradua-o)**
<br>
<a href="https://github.com/intcarlosmiguel/Programas-da-Gradua-o">
  <img src="https://miro.medium.com/max/1400/1*niydlv46lEZgSxIvKPAiwA.png" alt="Opinion Dynamics" align="right" width="25%" height="120"/>
</a>

> The proliferation of the internet has accelerated the spread of extreme opinions, misinformation, and polarised discourse — phenomena that intensified during the COVID-19 pandemic. This undergraduate research project simulates extreme opinion propagation models on **small-world network** topologies, with the goal of reproducing and analysing behaviours observed in real-world social networks. Simulations were implemented in **C** for computational efficiency.
>
> **Skills:** Network Science, Statistical Physics, C, Simulation
